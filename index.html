Abstract

In modern work environments, robots are required to perform tasks that demand high-precision 6D tracking tasks and versatile mobility. Wheeled Quadruped Manipulators (WQM) are particularly adept at meeting these demands, which expand their operational workspace with their legs and increase motion efficiency with their wheels, enabling both adaptive and effective movement. Loco-manipulation tasks needs the coordination between floating base and manipulator to fully exploit the capacity of the hardware. In this paper, we study the problem of loco-manipulation with the Wheeled Quadruped Manipulator (WQM) hardware. Our objective is to train a novel unified whole-body policy, which needs only 6D end-efector(EE) target commands from users. And our policy satisfies precise 6D EE pose tracking with safe and continuous locomotion. This policy autonomously transitions between locomotion and manipulation as needed. To fulfill this, a new reinforcement learning (RL) structure, named the Reward Fusion Module (RFM), is proposed. RFM can establish priority between tasks, switch modes between locomotion and manipulation, do the reward enhancement, and conduct cumulative penalty to achieve precise tracking. Finally, we initiatively bridge the gap of sim2real, and deploy our methods on the WQM platform both in simulation and reality to validate the feasibility and efficiency. The experiments demonstrate a smooth and precise tracking performance, and seamless switch between wheeled and legged to fully exploit efficiency.

Framework

In this paper, we use reinforcement learning to tackle whole-body loco-manipulation with the WQM platform, aiming to track a six-dimensional EE target with a single policy. As shown in the figure, our control architecture features a unified RL policy that takes long-short history observations and 6D EE commands as input. A student encoder processes the long history observations. The policy output is sent to a PD controller.
