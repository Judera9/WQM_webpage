Abstract

In modern work environments, robots are required to perform tasks that demand high-precision 6D tracking tasks and versatile mobility. Wheeled Quadruped Manipulators (WQM) are particularly adept at meeting these demands, which expand their operational workspace with their legs and increase motion efficiency with their wheels, enabling both adaptive and effective movement.

Loco-manipulation tasks require a good floating base to do the locomotion, a manipulator to do precise manipulation, and most importantly, coordination between them to fully exploit the capacity of the hardware. In this paper, we study the problem of loco-manipulation with the Wheeled Quadruped Manipulator (WQM) hardware. Our objective is to train a novel unified whole-body policy, which needs only 6D end-efector(EE) target commands from users. And our policy satisfies precise 6D EE pose tracking with safe and continuous locomotion. This policy autonomously transitions between locomotion and manipulation as needed.

To fulfill this, a new reinforcement learning (RL) structure named Reward Fusion Apparatus(RFA) is proposed. RFA can establish priority between tasks, switch modes between locomotion and manipulation, do the reward enhancement, and conduct cumulative penalty to achieve precise tracking. Finally, we deploy our methods on the WQM platform both in simulation and reality to validate the feasibility and efficiency.

The experiments demonstrate a smooth and precise tracking performance, and seamless switch between wheeled and legged to fully exploit efficiency.
